About

Dr. Aksana Ismailbekova is a distinguished research fellow at the Leibniz-Zentrum Moderner Orient (ZMO) in Berlin, Germany, where she has been conducting innovative research since January 2020. Her work focuses on the complex dynamics of kinship, ethnicity, patronage, conflict, and gender in Central Asia, with particular expertise in Kyrgyzstan.

Dr. Ismailbekova brings a unique perspective to her research, combining extensive fieldwork experience with rigorous academic training. Her scholarly contributions have significantly advanced our understanding of informal governance, corruption, and social dynamics in post-Soviet Central Asia.

Research Interests

Kinship & Patronage

Exploring the intricate networks of kinship and patronage systems in Central Asia and their impact on political and social structures.

Ethnicity & Conflict

Investigating ethnic dynamics and conflict resolution mechanisms in post-Soviet societies, particularly in Kyrgyzstan.

Gender Studies

Analyzing gender roles and women's experiences in Central Asian societies, including single mothers and their coping strategies.

Informal Governance

Studying informal governance structures, corruption patterns, and their intersection with formal political institutions.
